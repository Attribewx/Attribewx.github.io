My Level Design Process

Step One: Simple Block Out

I create a simple version of the level, focusing more on how the player will flow through it.

Spellforged

in Spellforged I use an app to draw out little level doodles to see if the level is a good idea before really cranking out the details.

Level Design: Blocking out level flow, Making use of mechanics and core gameplay.

Skills and Programs: Unity, C#, Photoshop.

Yahaha! You found me! -Korok

Step Two: Unity Blockout

Using Unity's Tilemap re-create the simple blockout, add enemy prefabs, and add other interactable objects.

Spellforged

In Spellforged, I use Unity's Tilemap system to blockout the shapes I made in the rough sketch, adding smaller platforms as well for more jumps and variety, while considering things such as jump difficulty, player risks(Health, money, position), and enemy pressure.

Level Design: Jump difficulty, Jump Decisions, Jump Risks

Yahaha! You found me! -Korok

Step Three: Add Lighting

Add scene lighting in the form of layers, such as global lights, lit objects like torches, and even glowing enemies.

Spellforged

In Spellforged I use light for multiple reasons, one reason is for making jumps they might have to do in dark or darker lighting, Scenery is more alive and dynamic with lighting, and it can be used for telegraphing and other timing effects.

Level Design: Dark Platforming, Dark Exploration, Enemy Telegraphing, Platforming Feedback(color indicators for platfoms)

Yahaha! You found me! -Korok

Step Four: Add Background Items

Add uninteractable items such as small rocks, trees, houses, and place objects that will render in front of the player immersing the camera into the world.

Spellforged

In Spellforged the last process I do is add background items. It's not a very difficult task but it's important to add them because it makes the game feel more full and believable. Objects I add are things such as stalactites, signs, and bushes which can bring the level together and make traversing through certain areas more memorable.

Level Design: Background Filling, Zone Distinction, Environmental Storytelling
